# 🎧 UX Refinement — Griot (Cinematic Phase)

## 🧠 Core Shift

We moved from:

❌ UI components  
→ lists, sections, buttons

To:

✅ Experience composition  
→ scene, emotion, presence

---

## 🎯 Key Principle

> The voice is the product.

Everything else must:

- support it
- not compete with it

---

## 🧱 Structural Changes

### 1. AudioCard → Scene

Before:

- multiple UI blocks
- stacked layout
- feature-driven

After:

- full-screen moment
- centered emotional text
- playback embedded in narrative

---

### 2. Hierarchy Fix

Old:

- mic + playback competing
- no clear primary action

New:

[VOICE (emotion)]
↓
[PLAYBACK (listen)]
↓
[SPACE]
↓
[MIC (create)]

---

### 3. Layered System

We introduced depth:

1. Content layer (scroll)
2. System layer (floating mic)
3. Feedback layer (toast)

This creates:
→ perceived product sophistication

---

## 🎙 Mic Philosophy

Initial attempt:

- glowing
- pulsing
- attention-heavy

Result:
❌ distracting
❌ competing with voice

Refined version:

- calm
- subtle
- reactive on touch only

> The mic waits. It does not shout.

---

## 🌊 Motion Guidelines

We learned:

- Motion should be felt, not seen
- Subtle > impressive
- Breathing animations can easily become noise

Current direction:

- minimal animation
- tactile feedback > visual loops

---

## 📊 Visual Stability

Problem:

- random waveform = visual noise

Fix:

- deterministic waveform pattern

Result:
→ feels intentional
→ feels designed

---

## 🧠 Mental Model

User experience flow:

1. Read (emotion)
2. Feel (pause)
3. Listen (tap)
4. Respond (mic)

No friction.
No confusion.

---

## ⚠️ What We Avoid

- Feature overload
- Loud animations
- Competing focal points
- “Cool UI” that breaks immersion

---

## 🧭 Product Identity Emerging

Griot is becoming:

→ not a social feed  
→ not a media app

But:

## 👉 a space of local voices

Quiet  
Real  
Human

---

## 🧪 Key Insight

The biggest improvements came from:

❌ removing  
not  
➕ adding
